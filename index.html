The Founder

Operator-led.
Relationship-driven.

Verdant is led by Nidhi Shah — an operator, not a consultant.

Over two decades, Nidhi has done the work that most market-entry firms only advise on — identifying the right customers, building credibility in unfamiliar markets, navigating the gap between global ambition and Indian execution reality. She has sat across the table from conservative buyers, worked through long sales cycles, and stayed through the difficult middle period when a technology is neither new nor established.

Verdant reflects her operator-led perspective — focused on market creation, technology adoption, and long-term value, not short-term transactions.

When you work with Verdant, you work with Nidhi directly.

"India is one of the world's most opportunity-rich and execution-intensive manufacturing markets. Success here requires more than market reports or channel partners. It requires operating experience, credibility, and a deep understanding of how decisions are made on the ground. Verdant exists to provide exactly that."

What Verdant is built on

Twenty years of starting conversations that didn't exist yet.

Before founding Verdant, Nidhi spent over two decades introducing industrial 3D printing across India's manufacturing sectors — from jewellery to foundries and from engineering to aerospace — in markets where there was very little awareness, no reference customers, and no established path forward.

~7.5 years
Imaginarium Solutions
3D Printing service bureau Mumbai, India

Part of the founding team, to introduce industrial 3D printing to India's jewellery and engineering sectors — at a time when the technology was largely unknown.

  • First 3 years - 1 to 6 industrial 3D printers; team expanded from 8 to 48
  • Diversified into automotive, aerospace, medical, foundry and engineering verticals
  • Led India manufacturing partnerships with two of the largest 3D Printing service bureaus in EU.
  • Headed the launch of Imaginarium's 3D printer sales division, with first OEM representation for RapidShape GmbH in India
~12 years
voxeljet AG (now part of ExOne Global Holdings)
3D Printing OEM Germany

Led India operations to bring large-format industrial 3D printing to Indian foundries — the most cost-sensitive and conservative segment of the manufacturing industry.

Later extended responsibility to ASEAN and ANZ markets.

  • Set up and ran voxeljet's wholly owned India subsidiary, including regulatory and compliance foundations
  • Installed 7 large-frame industrial 3D printing systems across Indian foundries
  • Established distributor networks and market visibility across ASEAN and ANZ
  • Part of the senior management team through a period of significant international growth
Philosophy

India is not
a single market. It never was.

It is a collection of industries, regions, ownership structures, and decision-making cultures. Verdant's approach reflects this complexity — grounded in operating reality, not theoretical frameworks.

Success in India is rarely fast — but it is durable when done right.

India market services

India market entry & scale-up

Engagements span the full market-entry and scale-up lifecycle:

  • Go-to-market and pricing localisation
  • Partner and distributor identification
  • Early customer access and reference development
  • Ongoing market development and scaling

Distribution & representation

Verdant selectively represent high value, differentiated manufacturing technologies and industrial solutions in India.

  • Acting as India distributor or commercial representative
  • Building and managing key accounts
  • Developing long-term sales pipelines
  • Positioning offerings for Indian buying behaviour and procurement structures

Representation mandates are chosen carefully, with a clear focus on long-term market creation rather than transactional sales.

Buyer-seller connectivity

Verdant connect the right buyers and sellers across borders, with clear commercial intent.

  • Global suppliers engaging with Indian OEMs and Tier 1 manufacturers
  • Indian manufacturers accessing global customers or technology partners
  • Structured introductions aligned on readiness, relevance, and mutual value

Connectivity is selective and purpose-driven — not volume-based.
